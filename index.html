How these estimates are calculated

Each year before retirement, the planner applies the selected investment return and adds 12 monthly contributions. Contributions rise with the income-growth assumption. The optional expense rises with inflation and is deducted at the chosen age.

At retirement, the plan target equals first-year retirement spending divided by 4% (25 times spending). After retirement, the chart applies investment growth, then subtracts spending that rises with inflation. Values are estimates in future dollars.
